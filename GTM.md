# Go-to-market

## Positioning

**One-liner:** “Mint meets McKinsey for AI subscriptions — a 60-second stack audit that names real waste.”

**ICP:** Seed–Series B engineering-heavy startups paying for multiple AI surfaces (IDE assistants, team chat SKUs, API commits).

## Acquisition loops

1. **Product-led:** Public `/audit/:shareId` links with OG previews that quote annualized savings.
2. **Founder social proof:** Landing uses mocked but labeled stats (“Teams saved $2.4K/mo (mocked)”) until production telemetry exists.
3. **Consult wedge:** If modeled monthly savings exceed **$500**, surface an aggressive “Talk to Credex” CTA; below **$100**, reinforce trust (“stack already lean”).

## Channels

- X / LinkedIn threads comparing “Current vs Better” SKUs (table already on landing).
- YC / indie Slack drops with a single CTA: `/audit`.
- Optional outbound: attach anonymized `shareId` links in cold email for social proof.

## Data capture

- Email **after** numbers render.
- Store `team_size` on the lead row to prioritize outbound sizing.

## Founder-facing narrative (landing + share)

- Lead with **pain** (silent budget line, duplicate SKUs) before mechanics.  
- **Common waste patterns** card grid is intentionally concrete — it pre-answers “is this toy?” in the first scroll.  
- **Trusted-by** strip stays mocked and labeled; real logos require legal clearance — assignment allows illustrative proof.  
- **Conditional honesty** on the results page (low savings = no fake urgency) is part of GTM: it increases reply quality on outbound.
